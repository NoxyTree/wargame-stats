01 Setting Up the Bot
1
Add the bot to your Discord server
Click the Add to Discord button and select your server. You'll need the Manage Server permission to install bots.
2
Create a stats channel
Create a dedicated channel for match logging — something like #wargame-stats or #match-log. This keeps results separate from general chat.
3
Import your roster (optional)
Run the import command to load your guild's player list. This helps the bot correctly identify which players belong to your guild versus allied mercenaries.
!wgimportroster
4
Log your first match
You're ready to go. See the guide below for how to log a match.
02 Logging Your First Match
1
Take a screenshot of the end-of-match scoreboard
At the end of each wargame, screenshot the full scoreboard showing all players, their damage dealt, defeats, assists, and healed amounts. Multiple screenshots are fine if the scoreboard doesn't fit in one.
2
Post the result in your stats channel
In your stats channel, type the match result and attach your screenshot(s). The format is:
!wgadd <type> vs <enemy> (We won/lost <map>)
3
The bot reads the scoreboard
Wargame Stats reads the player stats from your screenshot automatically. Within a few seconds it confirms the match has been logged with a full scoreboard summary.
4
Stats appear on the dashboard
Your guild's web dashboard updates immediately. Player totals, win rates, and leaderboards all reflect the new match.
03 Using the Dashboard
—
Overview tab
Shows your guild's win/loss record across all time, last 30 days, and last 7 days. The current win or loss streak is shown as a live card. Top performers are listed by average damage, kills, assists, and healing over the last 14 days.
—
Player Stats tab
The full roster leaderboard. Toggle between all-time totals, last 30 days, and last 7 days using the period buttons. Click any player row to open their full profile — per-game averages and complete match history.
—
Mercenaries & Feuds tabs
Mercenaries are allied players from outside your guild who appeared in your matches. Feuds are enemy players. Both tabs show per-game averages and are clickable for individual profiles.
—
Calendar tab
A monthly view of all your matches. Click any match event to open the full scoreboard. Use the arrows to navigate between months.
04 Command Reference
!wgadd Log a match. Attach one or more scoreboard screenshots and include the result in the message format shown above.
!lastgame Prints the full scoreboard from the most recently logged match directly in Discord.
!stats Overall win/loss record for the guild.
!statsenemy Win/loss breakdown per enemy guild, sorted by most games played.
!statsmap Win/loss breakdown per map.
!statstype Win/loss breakdown per game type.
!mystats <name> Pulls cumulative stats for a player by name. Supports partial and fuzzy name matching.
!wgwinrate <name> Shows a player's win/loss record and win rate. Supports partial name matching.
!wgtop <stat> Top 10 players by a given stat. Valid options: defeats, assists, damage, tanked, heals.
!wgimportroster Imports your guild's player roster to improve name recognition accuracy across all stat pipelines.
